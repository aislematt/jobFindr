Interview Prep Quick Reference

"Tell Me About Yourself" Script ▼

Opening (15 sec): "I'm a brand director with 12+ years in consumer healthcare, most recently leading innovation and incubation at Haleon where I managed brands across a $500M+ portfolio."

Bridge (15 sec): "What excites me about [women's health / mental health] is that it combines my deep experience in consumer healthcare marketing with a mission I'm personally passionate about. I've spent my career making health products accessible to consumers — from managing Rx-to-OTC switches to launching new brands."

Why This Role (15 sec): "I'm looking for a role where I can bring that same blend of strategic rigor and creative brand-building to a company that's transforming how people access [women's / mental] healthcare. That's exactly what drew me to [Company]."

Key Talking Points Mapped to Experience ▼
  • P&L Ownership: "I've had full P&L ownership of a $500MM+ brand portfolio, driving both top-line growth and margin improvement. I understand what it means to be accountable for business results, not just marketing metrics."
  • Innovation & Speed: "At Haleon's innovation incubator, I led 10+ product launches from concept to shelf in compressed timelines — essentially running a startup inside a large organization."
  • Rx-to-OTC Switch: "I led the marketing strategy for Rx-to-OTC transitions, which requires navigating FDA regulations, physician education, and consumer awareness simultaneously — very similar to the regulatory landscape in femtech and digital health."
  • Consumer-First Thinking: "My approach has always been consumer-first. I've led research programs, built segmentation frameworks, and translated insights into campaigns that drove measurable trial and loyalty."
  • Digital Commerce: "I've built and scaled DTC and e-commerce channels, which is core to how most health-tech companies acquire and retain customers."
Questions to Ask Interviewers ▼

For Startups (Series A-C):

  • "What does the current marketing function look like, and what would you want it to look like in 12 months?"
  • "What's the company's current customer acquisition mix, and where do you see the biggest opportunity?"
  • "How does the marketing team work with product and clinical teams?"
  • "What's your runway, and how does that inform your growth strategy?"

For Established Companies:

  • "What's the biggest marketing challenge the team is facing right now?"
  • "How is marketing's success measured at the executive level?"
  • "What's the relationship between brand marketing and performance marketing here?"
  • "Where do you see the biggest white space for brand growth in the next 2-3 years?"

Negotiation tips:

  • Always negotiate — 70% of employers expect it.
  • Lead with your P&L track record: "I've managed $500MM+ in brand P&L, which directly translates to..."
  • For startups, negotiate equity separately from base. Ask about vesting schedule, strike price, and latest 409A.
  • If base is firm, negotiate signing bonus, annual bonus target, title, or remote flexibility.
  • Benchmark: For NYC-based roles, aim for the upper range. Remote roles may be 5-10% lower but save on commute/relocation.

Job Search Strategy

Personalized tips based on your background as a Brand Director in consumer healthcare.

  • Lead with your P&L story: Managing a $500MM+ portfolio with full P&L ownership is rare and highly valued. Femtech startups scaling from Series B+ desperately need leaders who've managed at this scale. Frame your experience as "I've driven commercial growth for healthcare brands at scale and now want to apply that to a mission I'm passionate about."
  • Your Rx-to-OTC switch experience is gold: Many femtech/women's health companies are navigating FDA regulations, DTC strategies, and consumer education - exactly what Rx-to-OTC switch work involves. Highlight this unique expertise.
  • Innovation accelerator = startup DNA: Your incubator work at Haleon shows you can operate in ambiguous, fast-moving environments. This directly addresses the #1 concern hiring managers at startups have about big-company candidates.
  • Network in the FemTech ecosystem: Follow and engage with In Women's Health newsletter, join FemTech Focus and Women in Healthcare groups on LinkedIn. These communities regularly share unlisted roles.
  • Consider fractional/advisory roles: Your background advising minority and female entrepreneurs makes you a strong candidate for fractional CMO or advisory board positions at early-stage femtech companies. These often convert to full-time roles.
  • Your Citi finance background differentiates you: Few marketing leaders can credibly discuss P&L, unit economics, and CAC/LTV with a finance-first lens. This is a major differentiator, especially for VC-backed startups where investors want financially-savvy marketing leaders.
  • Set up LinkedIn job alerts: Create alerts for: "marketing director women's health", "VP marketing femtech", "brand director mental health", "head of marketing healthcare startup". Check daily.
  • Target headhunters: Healthcare-specialized recruiters like Storm3 focus on healthtech/mental health placements at the director+ level. Reach out proactively.
