Job Search Strategy

Personalized tips based on your background as a Brand Director in consumer healthcare.

  • Lead with your P&L story: Managing a $500MM+ portfolio with full P&L ownership is rare and highly valued. Femtech startups scaling from Series B+ desperately need leaders who've managed at this scale. Frame your experience as "I've driven commercial growth for healthcare brands at scale and now want to apply that to a mission I'm passionate about."
  • Your Rx-to-OTC switch experience is gold: Many femtech/women's health companies are navigating FDA regulations, DTC strategies, and consumer education - exactly what Rx-to-OTC switch work involves. Highlight this unique expertise.
  • Innovation accelerator = startup DNA: Your incubator work at Haleon shows you can operate in ambiguous, fast-moving environments. This directly addresses the #1 concern hiring managers at startups have about big-company candidates.
  • Network in the FemTech ecosystem: Follow and engage with In Women's Health newsletter, join FemTech Focus and Women in Healthcare groups on LinkedIn. These communities regularly share unlisted roles.
  • Consider fractional/advisory roles: Your background advising minority and female entrepreneurs makes you a strong candidate for fractional CMO or advisory board positions at early-stage femtech companies. These often convert to full-time roles.
  • Your Citi finance background differentiates you: Few marketing leaders can credibly discuss P&L, unit economics, and CAC/LTV with a finance-first lens. This is a major differentiator, especially for VC-backed startups where investors want financially-savvy marketing leaders.
  • Set up LinkedIn job alerts: Create alerts for: "marketing director women's health", "VP marketing femtech", "brand director mental health", "head of marketing healthcare startup". Check daily.
  • Target headhunters: Healthcare-specialized recruiters like Storm3 focus on healthtech/mental health placements at the director+ level. Reach out proactively.
